    The buying and selling of shares of publicly traded businesses can be done on stock exchanges, which are financial markets. These exchanges are usually run by private businesses, and their primary function is to make it easier for buyers and sellers to buy and sell securities. A stock exchange's main purpose is to act as a venue for the efficient and open trading of securities. Setting standards and guidelines for listing stocks, creating trading tools like electronic order matching, and giving investors access to real-time price information are all part of this.
